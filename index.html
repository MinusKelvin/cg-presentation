How Computer Graphics Work
An Interactive Presentation
Start
Your screen is a grid of pixels, and so all computer graphics must be put of a grid of pixels somehow.

The screen is basically a coordinate system, and so locations are just a pair of coordinates.
Using whole-number coordinates, points fall exactly on pixels' centers and everything is really easy.

Drawing lines is simple, you just start at the beginning of the line and step along it until you reach the end of the line.
Drawing filled shapes, such as triangles, is a little bit harder, but is still relatively simple to do quickly and efficently.
We can rotate these around the center of the screen with a little bit of math and...
Uh oh! That rotation is really choppy and not very smooth. Rather ugly.

The problem here is that we're losing information about the exact location of the triangle's corners.
Ah! Much better. By changing our coordinate system from using whole numbers to using decimals, we can target parts of pixels instead of just the centers of them.

This makes drawing stuff more complicated, but looks so much nicer.
One color is kinda boring. Here's 3 colors!

We colored one point of the triangle red, another green, and the last blue. The colors are smoothly shifted from one to another inside the triangle.
