How Computer Graphics Work Start
A computer screen is a grid of pixels. Inside each pixel are three LEDs that emit different colors of light. There is a red one, a green one, and a blue one. Together these produce all the colors you can see on a computer screen.
Here is a grid of pixels. Shapes can be drawn by filling in all the pixels inside of the shape. Here you can see a triangle rotating in the grid. There's a few problems with this, though. First of all, the triangle isn't rotated very smoothly.
